GIS Experience

The GIS Certificate requires seven courses in GIS and remote sensing. After two introductory courses, I took Principles and Applications of Remote Sensing, and Forest Surveying. I will soon be taking Quantitative Geospatial Analysis, Satellite Image Analysis, and Programming for GIS. I'm well versed in ArcGIS products and I have experience with Google Earth Engine, MB-System and Qimera (Bathymetry), Envi software, and Potree

Bathymetry of Active Volcanos

Working with Dr. Jeff Beeson and William Chadwick of Oregon State University, I cleaned and processed raw sonar data to create bathymetric maps, then created difference grids for two active seamounts in the Mariana Back-Arc (Ahyi and NW Rota-1). This experience

  • Check out the Bathymetry!

Studying Precipitation in the Historic Record

Can we identify flash flood events using only precipitation data? I analyzed monthly precipitation data from 1924-2024 to try doing just that. It kind of worked!! This was my first venture into utilizing scripts to collect and analyze very large datasets.

  • Check out my study!

Campus Bathroom Survey

For a class project, we were tasked with collecting our own spatial data. Aiming to maximize responses, we sought public input on which campus bathrooms stink. The project involved Survey123, ArcGIS Online, some Arc Pro and some good old fashioned outreach. The survey received over 250 responses.
